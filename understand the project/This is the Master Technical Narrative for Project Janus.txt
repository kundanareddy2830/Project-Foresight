1. The Physics Mechanism (The "ZI" Operator)
We constructed our Hamiltonian using Pauli Operators. The specific term we biased was ZI.
Z (Pauli-Z): This operator measures the "spin" of a qubit. It distinguishes between State 0 (Energy +1) and State 1 (Energy -1).
I (Identity): This operator does nothing. It leaves the qubit alone.
ZI in Qiskit:
Qiskit reads from Right to Left (Little Endian).
So, ZI means:
I acts on Qubit 0 (The Right Qubit) $\rightarrow$ Leaves it at 0.
Z acts on Qubit 1 (The Left Qubit) $\rightarrow$ Pushes it to flip to 1.
2. The Biasing Effect (The "Push")
When the Classical Screener (QSVC) detects fraud, we add a Positive Bias to the ZI coefficient.
No Bias: The system relaxes to its natural state: 00.
Positive Bias: The system tries to minimize energy.
For the Z operator, the state 1 has lower energy than state 0 when the coefficient is positive.
Therefore, the bias forces Qubit 1 to flip to 1.

So, if we apply a strong bias:
Qubit 0: Stays 0 (Unchanged).
Qubit 1: Flips to 1 (Forced by Bias).
